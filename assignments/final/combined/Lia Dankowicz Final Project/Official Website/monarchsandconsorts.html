The Monarchs and Consorts of Sweden

House of Munsö


Erik Segersäll (the Victorious) was born around 945 and died in around 995. He was King of Sweden from around 970 until his death.

The specifics of Erik Segersäll's family are not clear, but he is known to have had at least one son, in future King Olof Skötkonung.


Olof Skötkonung was born around 980 and died in 1022. He was King of Sweden from around 995 to 1022.

Estrid of the Obotrites was born around 979 and died in 1035.

Estrid of the Obotrites was Olof Skötkonung's only wife. They were married around the year 1000 and had two children, including future King Anund Jakob.


Edla of Venden (unknown birth/death)

Edla of Venden was Olof Skötkonung's mistress prior to his marriage to Estrid. She and Olof had at least two children together, including future King Emund the Old.


Anund Jakob was born around July 25, 1008 and died in 1050. He was King of Sweden from around 1022 to 1050.

Gunnhildr (Guda, Gyda) Sveinsdóttir died around 1060.

Gunnhildr was the only wife of Anund Jakob. Though the sources are a little unclear, Anund seems to have had a daughter, also called Gyda/Gunnhildr/Guda, either the daughter of him and Gunnhildr or of another woman.


Emund the Old died around 1060. He was King of Sweden from around 1050 until his death in around 1060.

Emund's spouse is not known, but he is said to have had two children with her, including a daughter who is modernly referred to as Ingamoder, the Queen Consort of King Stenkil.


House of Stenkil


Stenkil died in 1066. He was King of Sweden from around 1060 to 1066 and the founder of the House of Stenkil.

After Stenkil's death, there is said to have been a brief period of war during which two men named Eric contended for the throne. After both Erics died, Stenkil's son Halsten became king.

Ingamoder was born around 1025.

The spouse of King Stenkil and daughter of King Emund the Old, her actual name is unknown. Her modern name, Ingamoder, translates to "Mother of Inge." She had at least two children with Stenkil, including future Kings Inge the Elder and Halsten.


Halsten was born around 1050 and died after 1081. He was the sole King of Sweden from around 1067 to around 1070 and is said to have ruled jointly with his brother Inge the Elder from around 1079 until his death.

There is little information about the ruler(s) of Sweden in between Halsten's two reigns.

Emund's spouse is not known, but he is known to have had two children, the future co-Monarchs Filip and Inge the Younger


Inge the Elder's exact date of death is unknown but was likely between 1105 and 1110. He was King of Sweden from around 1079 to around 1083 (for a time, alongside his brother Halsten) and again from around 1085 until his death.

In 1083, Inge was briefly deposed and replaced by Blot-Sven, who ruled for "three winters" before being killed by Inge who then reclaimed the throne.

Helena (Elin) was born in the 11th Century and died in the early 12th Century.

During their marriage, Helena and Inge had four children, including a future Queen Consort of Norway and a future Queen Consort of Norway and Denmark (through two marriages).


Blot-Sven died around 1085. He was King of Sweden from around 1083 until he was killed by Inge the Elder in 1085.


Filip died around 118. He was King of Sweden alongside his brother Inge the Younger from around 1110 (after Inge the Elder's death) until his death.

Filip was married to Ingegard of Norway, the daughter King Harald Hardrada of Norway. They had no known children.


Inge the Younger was King of Sweden alongside his brother Filip from around 1110 to around 1118 and then ruled solely after Filip's death in around 1118 until 1125.

Ulvhild Håkansdotter was born around 1095 and died around 1148 (between 1143 and 1150).

Ulvhild and Inge were married in around 1117 but had no surviving children. The marriage was Ulvhild's first; in her third she married another King of Sweden, Sverker I.


Ragnvald Knaphövde was briefly King of Sweden in the mid-1120s.

He was elected King in Uppland and recognized in Östergötland, but not in Västergötland, where he was murdered by supporters of Magnus I.


House of Estridsen


Magnus I (Magnus Nilsson) was born around 1106 and died on June 4, 1134. He was King of Sweden from the mid-1120s to around 1132.

He was not widely recognized as King of Sweden at time, only officially ruling the southern region of Götaland (Gothenland).

Richeza (Rikissa) was born on April 12, 1116 and died after December 25, 1156.

Richeza and Magnus married around 1127 and had two sons. This marriage was Richeza's first; in her third, she married another King of Sweden, Sverker I.


House of Sverker


Sverker (I) the Elder was murdered on December 25, 1156. He was King of Sweden from around 1132 until 1156 and was the founder of the House of Sverker.

Ulvhild Håkansdotter was born around 1095 and died around 1148 (between 1143 and 1150).

Ulvhild and Sverker were married in around 1134 and had four children, including the future King Karl Sverkersson (Charles VII). Ulvhild was the widow of King Inge the Younger.


Richeza was born on April 12, 1116 and died after December 25, 1156.

Richeza and Sverker were married in around 1148 (soon after Ulvhild's death) and had at least one son. Richeza was the widow of King Magnus I.


House of Erik


Erik (IX) the Pious was born around 1120-25 and died on May 18, 1160. He was King of Sweden from around 1156 to 1160 and the founder of the House of Erik.

Erik was assassinated, representing the factions in Sweden who supported other claimants to the throne. His reign did not include the region of Östergötland, which was ruled by future King Karl Sverkersson (Charles VII).

He was later venerated as Saint Erik in the Catholic and Lutheran Churches.

Christina (Kristina Björnsdotter) was born around 1120/25 and died around 1160/70.

Erik and Christina were married around 1140 and had four children, including future King Canute I (Knut Eriksson).


House of Stenkil


Magnus II (Magnus Henriksson) was born around 1130 and died in 1161. He was briefly King of Sweden from 1160 to 1161.

His reign did not include the region of Östergötland, which was ruled by future King Karl Sverkersson (Charles VII).

Birgitta Haraldsdotter was born around 1131 and died around 1208.

Birgitta and Magnus had no children.


House of Sverker


Karl Sverkersson (Charles VII) was born around 1130 and died on April 12, 1167. He was King of Sweden from around 1161 to 1167.

Karl became king of Östergötland around 1158, only gaining control of the rest of the country after killing Magnus II in battle in 1161.

He was assassinated by supporters of future King Canute I (Knut Eriksson) in 1167.

Christina (Kristina) was born around 1145 and died around 1200.

Karl and Kristina were married around 1163 and and had one son, the future King Sverker II.


House of Erik


Canute I (Knut Eriksson) was born before 1150 and died 1195/96. He was King of Sweden from around 1167 to 1195.

From 1167 to 1172/73, Knut fought for the throne against relatives of Sverker the Elder before eventually being able to claim the throne of the entire country.

The name of Knut's wife is not clear but the couple had five children, including the future King Erik Knutsson.


House of Sverker


Sverker II (Sverker Karlsson, Sverker the Younger) was born before 1167 and died on July 17, 1210. He was King of Sweden from 1195/96 to 1208.

Benedicta Ebbesdatter was born around 1165/1170 and died around 1199/1200.

Benedicta and Sverker were likely married in the mid-1180s and had at least one child.


Ingegerd Birgesdotter of Bjelbo was born around 1180 and died around 1210/1230.

Ingegerd and Sverker married in 1200 and had one son, the future King Johan Sverkersson (Johan/John I).


House of Erik


Erik (X) Knutsson was born around 1180 and died on April 10 1216. He was King of Sweden from around 1208 to 1216.

Richeza was born around 1190/91 and died in 1220.

Richeza and Erik married in around 1210 and had at least four children.


House of Sverker


Johan Sverkersson (Johan/John I) was born around 1201 and died on March 10, 1222. He was King of Sweden from around 1216 to 1222.

Johan died young, unmarried, and childless.


House of Erik


Erik Eriksson (the Lisp and Lame) was born in 1216 and died on February 2, 1250. He was King of Sweden from around 1222 to 1229 and again from 1234 to 1250.

He was the last Swedish monarch from the House of Erik.

Catherine (Karin, Katarina) was born around 1215 and died in 1252.

Catherine and Erik were married in 1243 or 1244 but had no surviving children.


House of Bjelbo (Folkunga)


Canute (II) the Tall (Knut Långe) died in 1234. He was King of Sweden from 1229 until his death.

Knut had two sons with an unkown spouse.


Birger Jarl (Birger Magnusson) was born around 1210 and died on October 21, 1266. He was Regent of Sweden from 1250 to 1266.

Ingeborg was born around 1212 and died on June 17, 1254.

Ingeborg and Birger were married around 1234 and had at least 8 children, including future Kings Valdemar and Magnus III (Magnus Ladulås).


Mechthild was born around 1220 or 1225 and died in 1288.

Mechthild and Birger were married in 1261 but had no confirmed children.


Valdemar was born in 1239 and died on December 26, 1302. He was King of Sweden from 1250 to 1275.

For the first 16 years of Valdemar's reign, his father and Regent, Birger Jarl was essentially the real ruler. After his father's death, Valdemar ruled on his own until being ousted by his younger brother Magnus in 1275.

Sophia (Sofia) was born in 1241 and died in 1286.

Sophia and Valdemar were married in 1260 and separated in 1277. They had six children together.


Jutta was born in 1246 and died in 1286/95.

Jutta was Sophia's sister, who had an affair with Valdemar in 1272, resulting in a son.


Magnus III (Magnus Ladulås) was likely born around 1240 and died on December 18, 1290. He was King of Sweden from 1275 to 1290.

Helvig was born before 1264 and died around 1324 or 1325.

Helvig and Magnus married in 1276 and had six children, including the future King Birger Magnusson.


Birger Magnusson was born in 1280 and died on May 31, 1321. He was King of Sweden from 1290 to 1318.

After capturing and imprisoning his younger brothers (rivals for the throne), resulting in their deaths, he was forced to flee to Denmark in 1318 and relinquish the throne.

Martha was born in 1277 and died on March 2, 1341.

Martha and Birger married in 1302 and had four children.


Magnus (IV) Eriksson was born in April or May of 1316 and died on December 1, 1374. He was King of Sweden from 1319 to 1364.

He ruled solely from 1319 to 1356 and from 1359 to 1362 and jointly with his son Erik Magnusson from 1356 to 1359 and his son Håkan Magnusson from 1362 to 1364.

Blanche (Blanka) was born in 1320 and died in 1363.

Blanche and Magnus married in 1335 and had three children, including future King Erik Magnusson.


Erik (XII) Magnusson was born in 1339 and died in June 21, 1359. He was King of Sweden from 1356 to 1359 alongside his father, Magnus (IV) Eriksson.

Beatrice (Beatrix) was born in 1344 and died on December 25, 1359.

Beatrice and Erik married in 1356, but had no surviving children.


Håkan (VI) Magnusson was born in 1340 and died in 1380. He was King of Sweden from 1362 to 1364 alongside his father, Magnus (IV) Eriksson.

Margareta was born on March 15, 1353 and died on October 28, 1412.

Margaret and Håkon married in 1363 and had one son. Margareta later became Queen of Sweden after deposing King Albrekt.


House of Mecklenburg


Albrekt the Younger was born around 1338 and died on April 1, 1412. He was King of Sweden from 1364 to 1389.

In 1389 Albrekt was defeated, captured, deposed, and imprisoned. He was released in 1395 and officially gave over control of Stockholm to Margareta in 1398.

Richardis was born around 1347 and died on April 23 or July 11, 1377.

Richardis and Albrekt were married in 1359 and had two children.


Agnes was born before 1356 and died between August 1, 1430 and December 22, 1434.

Agnes and Albrekt were married in 1396 and had one son. Agnes was not Queen Consort of Sweden.


House of Estridsen


Margareta was born and died on . She was Queen of Sweden from 1389 to 1396.

Margareta was the Queen Consort of King Håkan (VI) Magnusson(died in 1380) with whom she had one son who died in 1387. She founded the Kalmar Union as Queen of Denmark, Sweden, and Norway.


Erik of Pomerania (Erik XIII) was born in 1381 or 1382 and died on September 24, 1459. He was King of Sweden from 1396 to 1439.

He was by birth of the House of Griffin, but adopted into the House of Estridsen. He was the second ruler of the Kalmar Union as King of Denmark, Sweden, and Norway and was deposed in 1439.

Philippa was born on June 4, 1394 and died on January 5, 1430.

Philippa and Erik married in 1406 and had no children.


Cecilia died after 1459.

Cecilia was intially Erik's mistress and later his morganatic wife, meaning they were of different social ranks and Erik's titles could not pass to Cecilia. Cecilia and Erik had no children.


House of Bonde


Karl Knutsson Bonde, later Karl VIII was born on October 5, 1409 and died on May 14, 1470. He was Regent from 1438 to 1440 and King of Sweden from 1448 to 1457, from 1464 to 1465, and from 1467 to 1470.

Birgitta died in 1436.

Birgitta and Karl married in 1428 and she died before he became Regent or King of Sweden. The couple had two children.


Catherine (Katarina) died on September 7, 1450.

Katarina and Karl married in 1438 and she became Queen Consort of Sweden alongside him in 1448. They had eight children, only four of which survived to adulthood.


Christina (Kristina) was born in 1432 and died in 1492.

Kristina was a mistress of Karl who he married in 1470 before his death, thereby making her Queen Consort of Sweden. They had two children together.


House of Palatinate-Neumarkt (part of the House of Wittelsbach)


Kristoffer of Bavaria was born on February 26, 1416 and died on January 5/6 1448. He was King of Sweden from 1441 to 1448.

Dorothea was born around 1430/1431 and died on November 10, 1495.

Dorothea and Kristoffer married in 1445 and had no children. Dorothea later married another King of Sweden, Kristian I.


House of Oldenburg


Kristian I was born in February, 1426 and died on May 21, 1481. He was King of Sweden from 1457 to 1464.

Dorothea was born around 1430/1431 and died on November 10, 1495.

Dorothea and Kristian married in 1449 and had five children, including future King Hans (Johan II).


House of Sture


Sten Sture the Elder was born in 1440 and died on December 14, 1503. He was Regent of Sweden from 1470 to 1497 and again from 1501 to 1503.

Ingeborg died in December 1507.

Ingeborg and Sten married in 1467 and had no children.


House of Oldenburg


Hans (Johan II) was born on February 2, 1455 and died on February 20, 1513. He was King of Sweden from 1497 to 1501.

Christina was born on December 25, 1461 and died on December 8, 1521.

Christina and Johan married in 1478 and had five or six children, including future King Kristian II.


House of Natt och Dag


Svante Nilsson was born around 1460 and died on January 2, 1512. He was Regent of Sweden from 1504 to 1512.

Though sometimes referred to as Svante Nilsson Sture, the surname came from his mother's family and he never used it himself.

Iliana was born in 1468 and died in 1495.

Iliana and Svante married in 1486 and had one son, future Regent Sten Sture the Younger.


Mette was born around 1465 and died before 1533.

Mette and Svante married in 1504 and had no children. As his wife, Mette served as Regent Consort (de facto Queen Consort)


Sten Sture the Younger was born in 1493 and died on February 3, 1520. He was Regent of Sweden from 1512 to 1520.

Though officially of the House of Natt och Dag, he took on the surname of his great-grandmother, Sture, therefore becoming Sten Sture the Younger.

Sten died in February before the Stockholm Bloodbath in November, but Kristian II had Sten's corpse exhumed and burned nonetheless.

Christina (Kristina) was born in 1494 and died in January 1559.

Christina and Sten were married in 1511 and had six children.


House of Oldenburg


Kristian II was born on July 1, 1481 and died on January 25, 1559. He was King of Sweden from 1520 to 1521.

In Sweden, Kristian is known as Kristian the Tyrant, due to his execution of 82 noblemen in what is called the Stockholm Bloodbath. The bloodbath led to Sweden's exit from the Kalmar Union and Kristian's loss of the Swedish throne.

Isabella was born on July 18, 1501 and died on January 19, 1526.

Isabella and Kristian were married by proxy in 1514 and the marriage was ratified in 1515. They had six children, only two of which survived to adulthood.


House of Vasa


Gustav I Eriksson (Vasa) was born on May 12, 1496 and died on September 29, 1560. He was Regent from 1521 to 1523 and King of Sweden from 1523 to 1560.

Motto: "All Power is of God"

→ His official page on the Royal Palaces website.

He became King after defeating Kristian II in the Swedish War of Liberation. During this time, from 1521-23, he referred to himself as the Protector or Regent of Sweden.

Gustav replaced the elctive monarchy of Sweden at the time with a hereditary monarchy, which continues to this day.

Catherine was born on September 24, 1513 and died on September 23, 1535.

→ Her official page on the Royal Palaces website.

Catherine and Gustav married in 1531 and had one son, the future King Erik XIV.


Margareta was born on January 1, 1516 and died on August 26, 1551.

→ Her official page on the Royal Palaces website.

Margareta and Gustav married in 1536 and had 10 children, including future Kings Johan III and Karl IX.


Katarina was born on July 22, 1535 and died on December 13, 1621.

→ Her official page on the Royal Palaces website.

Katarina and Gustav married in 1552 and had no children.


Erik XIV was born on December 13, 1533 and died on February 26, 1577. He was King of Sweden from 1560 to 1568.

Motto: "God gives to whom He will"

→ His official page on the Royal Palaces website.

Erik went insane over time and was eventually deposed after he murdered several members of the Sture family in 1567 in what is called the Sture Murders. In September 1568 his brother Johan took control following a rebellion of the nobility. There continued to be support of him among the Swedish people but died as a prisoner of arsenic poisoning in 1577.

Karin was born on November 6, 1550 and died on September 13, 1612.

→ Her official page on the Royal Palaces website.

Karin and Gustav married in 1568 and had four children.


Agda died after 1565.

Agda was Erik's official royal mistress from 1558 to 1561 and had three children with him.


Johan (John) III was born on December 20, 1537 and died on November 17, 1592. He was King of Sweden from 1568 to 1592.

Motto: "God our Defender"

→ His official page on the Royal Palaces website.

Catherine (Katarina) was born on November 1, 1526 and died on September 16, 1583.

→ Her official page on the Royal Palaces website.

Catherine and Johan married in 1562 and had three children, including future King Sigismund.


Gunilla was born on June 25, 1568 and died on July 19, 1597.

→ Her official page on the Royal Palaces website.

Gunilla and Johan married in 1585 and had a son.


Karin was born in 1539 and died in 1596.

Karin was Johan's official royal mistress from 1555 to 1562 and had four children with him.


Sigismund III Vasa was born on June 20, 1566 and died on April 30, 1632. He was King of Sweden from 1592 to 1599.

Motto: "For Justice and the People"

→ His official page on the Royal Palaces website.

Sigismund was deposed in 1599 and his uncle, Karl IX, became King in his place.

Anne was born on August 16, 1573 and died on February 10, 1598.

→ Her official page on the Royal Palaces website.

Anne and Sigismund married in 1592 and had five children.


Constance was born on December 24, 1588 and died on July 10, 1631.

Constance and Sigismund were married in 1605 and had seven children. She was Queen of Poland, but not Sweden, as Sigismund was deposed before their marriage.


Karl IX was born on October 4, 1550 and died on October 30, 1611. He was Regent from 1599 to 1604 and King of Sweden from 1604 to 1611.

Motto: "In God I trust"

→ His official page on the Royal Palaces website.

Anna Maria was born on July 24, 1561 and died on July 29, 1589.

Anna Maria and Karl married in 1579 and had six children together. She died before he became King.


Christina was born on April 13, 1573 and died on December 8, 1625.

→ Her official page on the Royal Palaces website.

Christina and Karl married in 1592 and had five children (including one stillborn son), including future King Gustav II Adolf (Gustavus Adolphus).


Karin was born around 1551 and died in 1613.

Karin was Karl's mistress between 1568 and 1578 and had one son with him.


Gustav II Adolf (Gustavus Adolphus) was born on December 19, 1594 and died on November 6, 1632. He was King of Sweden from 1611 to 1632.

Motto: "With God and arms victorious"

→ His official page on the Royal Palaces website.

Maria Eleonora was born on November 11, 1599 and died on March 28, 1655.

→ Her official page on the Royal Palaces website.

Maria Eleonora and Gustav Adolf married in 1620 and had four children, three of which died young, the fourth of which was future Queen Kristina (Christina).


Margareta died in 1669.

Margareta was Gustav Adolf's mistress and had one son with him.


Kristina (Christina) was born on December 18 (Old Style: December 8), 1626 and died on April 19, 1689. She was Queen of Sweden from 1632 to 1654.

Motto: "Wisdom is the prop of the realm"

→ Her official page on the Royal Palaces website.

Kristina abdicated the throne in 1654 after converting to Catholicism. She never married and had no children.


House of Palatinate-Zweibrücken (or Pfalz, part of the House of Wittelsbach)


Karl X Gustav was born on November 8, 1622 and died on February 13, 1660. He was King of Sweden from 1654 to 1660.

Motto: "In God my destiny - He shall perform it"

→ His official page on the Royal Palaces website.

Hedwig Eleonora was born on October 23, 1636 and died on November 24, 1715.

→ Her official page on the Royal Palaces website.

Hedwig Eleonor and Karl Gustav married in 1654 and had one son, the future King Karl XI.


Märta was born in 1628 and died before 1677.

Märta was Karl's mistress and had one son with him.


Karl XI was born on November 24, 1655 and died on April 5, 1697. He was King of Sweden from 1660 to 1697.

Motto: "The Lord is become my Defender"

→ His official page on the Royal Palaces website.

Ulrika Eleonora was born on September 11, 1656 and died on July 26, 1693.

→ Her official page on the Royal Palaces website.

Ulrika Eleonora and Karl married in 1680 and had seven children including future King Karl XII and future Queen Ulrika Eleonora (the Younger).


Karl XII was born on June 17, 1682 and died on November 30, 1718. He was King of Sweden from 1697 to 1718.

Motto: "With God's Help"

→ His official page on the Royal Palaces website.

He never married and had no children. He was succeeded by his sister, Ulrika Eleonora (the Younger).


Ulrika Eleonora (the Younger) was born on January 23, 1688 and died on November 24, 1741. She was Queen of Sweden from 1718 to 1720.

Motto: "In God my Hope"

→ Her official page on the Royal Palaces website.

Ulrika Eleonora became Queen after her brother's death, eventually abdicating so her husband could become King in 1720.

Frederick I was born on April 28, 1676 and died on April 5, 1751.

→ His official page on the Royal Palaces website.

Ulrika Eleonora and Frederick married in 1715 and had no children.


House of Hesse


Frederick I was born on April 28, 1676 and died on April 5, 1751. He was King of Sweden from 1720 to 1751.

Motto: "In God my Hope"

→ His official page on the Royal Palaces website.

Louise (Luise) Dorothea was born on September 29, 1680 and died on December 23, 1705.

Luise Dorothea and Frederick married in 1700. Luise Dorothea died in childbirth.


Ulrika Eleonora was born on January 23, 1688 and died on November 24, 1741.

Ulrika was the Queen of Sweden from 1718 to 1720 before abdicating the throne to Frederick. The couple married in 1715 and had no children.

→ Her official page on the Royal Palaces website.


Hedvig was born on October 31, 1714 and died on February 11, 1744.

Hedvig was Frederick's official royal mistress from 1731 to 1744 and had at least three children.


House of Holstein-Gottorp (part of the House of Oldenburg)


Adolf Fredrik was born on May 14, 1710 and died on February 12, 1771. He was King of Sweden from 1751 to 1771.

Motto: "The state's welfare is my welfare"

→ His official page on the Royal Palaces website.

Lovisa Ulrika (Louisa Ulrika) was born on July 24, 1720 and died on July 16, 1782.

→ Her official page on the Royal Palaces website.

Lovisa Ulrika and Adolf Fredrik married in 1744 and had five children, including future Kings Gustav III and Karl XIII.


Gustav III was born January 24, 1746 and died on March 29, 1792. He was King of Sweden from 1771 to 1792.

Motto: "The Fatherland"

→ His official page on the Royal Palaces website.

Gustav made it legal for Catholics and Jews to live in Sweden.

Sophia (Sofia) Magdalena was born on July 3, 1746 and died on August 21, 1813.

→ Her official page on the Royal Palaces website.

Sofia Magdalena and Gustav married in 1766 and had two sons, including future King Gustav IV Adolf.


Gustav IV Adolf was born on November 1, 1778 and died on February 7, 1837. He was King of Sweden from 1792 to 1809.

Motto: "God and the People"

→ His official page on the Royal Palaces website.

A coup in 1809 led to Gustav Adolf's imprisonment alongside his family and his subsequent abdication. His uncle Karl XIII claimed the throne in June.

Frederica (Fredrika) was born on March 12, 1781 and died on September 25, 1826.

→ Her official page on the Royal Palaces website.

Fredrika married Gustav Adolf in 1797 and had five children together. They divorced in 1812.


Karl XIII was born on October 7, 1748 and died on February 5, 1818. He was King of Sweden from 1809 to 1818.

Motto: "The welfare of the people is my highest law"

→ His official page on the Royal Palaces website.

Hedvig Elisabet Charlotta was born on March 22, 1759 and died on June 20, 1818.

Charlotta and Karl married in 1774. They had two children who died in infancy.

→ Her official page on the Royal Palaces website.

In need of an heir to the throne, the couple first "adopted" Carl August, a Danish prince, who unfortunately died in 1810. After his death, they chose Jean Baptiste Bernadotte in his stead, who would become King Karl XIV Johan.


Augusta was born on March 10, 1754 and died on April 8, 1846.

Augusta had an affair with Karl before his marriage with Hedvig which resulted in one child, a son.


House of Bernadotte


Karl XIV Johan (Jean Baptiste Bernadotte) was born on January 26, 1763 and died on March 8, 1844. He was King of Sweden from 1818 to 1844.

Motto: "The love of the people my reward"

→ His official page on the Royal Palaces website.

Before becoming Crown Prince of Sweden, Karl Johan was a General in the French Army. He was the first monarch of the Bernadotte family.

Désirée (Desideria) was born on November 8, 1777 and died on December 17, 1860.

→ Her official page on the Royal Palaces website.

Désirée and Karl Johan married in 1798 and had one child, the future King Oskar I.


Oskar I was born on July 4, 1799 and died on July 8, 1859. He was King of Sweden from 1844 to 1859.

Motto: "Justice and Truth"

→ His official page on the Royal Palaces website.

Josephine was born on March 14, 1807 and died on June 7, 1876.

→ Her official page on the Royal Palaces website.

Josephine and Oskar married in 1823 and had five children, including future Kings Karl XV and Oskar II.


Emilie was born on April 29, 1812 and died on December 18, 1846.

Emilie had an affair with Oskar which resulted in two sons.


Jacquette was born on July 4, 1797 and died on January 7, 1839.

Jacquette was Oskar's mistress around 1819-1827 and is believed to have had a daughter with him.


Karl XV was born on May 3, 1826 and died on September 18, 1872. He was King of Sweden from 1859 to 1872.

Motto: "By law the land shall be built"

→ His official page on the Royal Palaces website.

As he had no legitimate male heir for the throne, it was passed to his brother, Oskar II, after his death.

Louise was born on August 5, 1828 and died on March 30, 1871.

→ Her official page on the Royal Palaces website.

Louise and Karl married in 1850 and had two children, a son, who died young, and a daughter, who became Queen Consort of Denmark.


Oskar II was born on January 21, 1829 and died on December 8, 1907. He was King of Sweden from 1872 to 1907.

Motto: "For the good of the kindred nations → For Sweden's good"

→ His official page on the Royal Palaces website.

Sophia was born on July 9, 1836 and died on December 30, 1913.

→ Her official page on the Royal Palaces website.

Sophia and Oskar married in 1857 and had four sons, including future King Gustaf V.


Gustaf V was born on June 16, 1858 and died on October 29, 1950. He was King of Sweden from 1907 to 1950.

Motto: "With the people for the Fatherland"

→ His official page on the Royal Palaces website.

He started the current practice of Swedish kings not having a coronation.

Victoria was born on August 7, 1862 and died on April 4, 1930.

→ Her official page on the Royal Palaces website.

Victoria and Gustaf married in 1881 and had three children, including future King Gustaf VI Adolf.


Gustaf VI Adolf was born on November 11, 1882 and died on September 15, 1973. He was King of Sweden from 1950 to 1973.

Motto: "Duty above all"

→ His official page on the Royal Palaces website.

Margaret was born on January 15, 1882 and died on May 1, 1920.

Margaret and Gustaf Adolf married in 1905 and had five children. Margaret died before Gustaf Adolf became King in 1950, but became Crown Princess of Sweden in 1907 alongside her husband.


Louise was born on July 13, 1889 and died on March 7, 1965.

→ Her official page on the Royal Palaces website.

Louise and Gustaf Adolf married in 1923 but had no surviving children.


Carl XVI Gustaf was born on April 30, 1946. He became King of Sweden in 1973.

Motto: "For Sweden - With the Times"

→ His official page on the Royal Palaces website.

Carl Gustaf passed a law in 1980, making his firstborn, Victoria, his heir apparent through absolute primogeniture.

Silvia was born on December 23, 1943.

→ Her official page on the Royal Palaces website.

Silvia and Carl Gustaf married in 1976 and have three children and seven grandchildren.
